K9 Police Officer

Description

A K9 Police Officer works alongside specially trained dogs to enhance law enforcement operations, including narcotics detection, search and rescue missions, suspect apprehension, and crowd control.

Average Salary

The average salary for a K9 Police Officer varies based on location, experience, and the specific department, typically ranging from $40,000 to $80,000 per year.

Employers

  • City Police Departments
  • County Sheriff's Offices
  • Federal Agencies (e.g., FBI, DEA)
  • Private Security Firms

Detailed Information

K9 Police Officers handle and care for police dogs, that are specially trained for a variety of tasks, which include guarding and search and rescue and stopping fleeing suspects.
